Informed Consent for Chiropractic Examination and Care

Cafe of Life Chiropractic & Wellness is committed to a shared decision-making process regarding your health. You have the right to be informed about your condition, the recommended care and treatment, and to decide whether to receive care with full knowledge of its known benefits and risks. This information is provided to help you make an informed decision to give or withhold your consent.

Chiropractic care is based on the science of the relationship between spinal structure and nervous system function, and how restoring proper alignment supports the body's natural ability to heal. Vertebral subluxation — a condition in which one or more vertebrae are misaligned or do not move properly — may cause interference or irritation to the nervous system. The primary goal of chiropractic care is the removal or reduction of this nerve interference.

A chiropractic examination may include spinal and physical assessment, orthopedic and neurological testing, palpation, specialized instrumentation, radiological examination (x-rays), and laboratory testing. The chiropractic adjustment is the application of a precise, controlled movement to the spine or joints to reduce or correct subluxation(s). Adjustments are typically delivered by hand; some may involve the use of an instrument or specialized equipment. Physiotherapy or rehabilitative procedures may also be included in the management protocol.

Benefits may include reduced pain, increased mobility, and improved quality of life.

Risks, though uncommon, may include temporary soreness, musculoskeletal sprain/strain, or fracture. Physiotherapy risks may additionally include allergic reaction and muscle or joint pain. There is a reported association between cervical manipulation and stroke in rare cases; however, current research and scientific evidence does not establish a causal relationship between chiropractic care and stroke. Rather, recent studies indicate that patients may seek care while already in the early stages of a stroke. You are being informed of this association because a stroke may cause serious neurological impairment.
